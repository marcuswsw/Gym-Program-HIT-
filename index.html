Key principles
One working set
1–2 escalating warm-up sets, then one all-out set taken beyond failure.
Rest-pause
Hit failure, rack, breathe 10–15 sec, squeeze out 2–3 more reps.
Slow negatives
3–4 sec eccentric on all exercises, especially isolation work.
Log everything
Hit the top of the rep range? The app tells you to add weight next session.
45–60 min max
High intensity, no socialising. Leave when the work is done.
Progressive overload
Hit top of rep range → add weight next session. That is the whole game.

Monday
Back
Open with the pullover to establish the lat connection before loading. Yates Row (underhand grip) is the primary mass builder. Weighted pullups close the lat work. Back extension finishes the erectors and glute-ham tie-in safely.
Lats — pre-exhaust
Cable pullover
1 × 12 warm-up
1 × 10–12 working
Pre-exhaustSlow neg
Initiate with the lats, not the arms. Full stretch overhead, hard squeeze at the bottom. Establishes the mind-muscle connection before any rowing.
Mark done
Lats — compound rowing
Yates Row (underhand barbell row)
1 × 10 warm-up
1 × 6–8 working
Rest-pauseSlow neg
Slight forward lean (~30°), underhand grip, drive elbows back past the torso. Explosive pull, 3-sec lower. Rest-pause: re-grip, breathe 10–12 sec, squeeze out 2–3 more. The primary mass builder.
Mark done
Seated Cable Row
1 × 8–10 working
Slow neg
Second rowing movement to fully inroad the mid-back and lats. Chest tall, drive elbows past the body, pause contracted.
Mark done
Lats — vertical pull
Weighted pullupsmust
1 × BW × 5 warm-up
1 × 6–8 working
Slow negRest-pause
Add a plate or dipping belt. Full hang at the bottom. Pull chest toward the bar, 3-sec lowering. Use a pulldown as working set if bodyweight reps are below 6 clean.
Mark done
Erectors + glute-ham tie-in
Loaded back extension
1 × 12 BW warm-up
1 × 10–12 working
Slow neg
Hold a plate to your chest. Lower 3–4 sec, drive to just past parallel — not hyperextended. Squeeze glutes hard at the top. Add weight to the plate over time.
Mark done
Tuesday
Deltoids + Triceps
Deltoids first while fresh. Lateral and rear heads are the priority — front delts get significant indirect work from Friday's pressing. Triceps finish the session.
Deltoids — pressing
Seated dumbbell press
1–2 × 10 warm-up
1 × 8–10 working
Rest-pauseSlow neg
Seated DB gives more ROM. Smith is safer at true failure solo. Controlled descent, drive through full range, rest-pause past failure.
Mark done
Deltoids — lateral + rear
Seated dumbbell lateral raise
1 × 12 warm-up
1 × 10–12 working
Slow negForced reps
Seated removes momentum — every rep is honest. Lead with the elbows, slight forward lean, slow 3-sec lowering. Forced reps extend past failure.
Mark done
Cable face pull
1 × 12–15 working
Slow neg
Full range, avoid traps taking over. Rear delts protect the rotator cuff under heavy pressing load and tie the shoulder to the back visually.
Mark done
Triceps
Cable pushdown
1 × 12 warm-up
1 × 10–12 working
Slow negRest-pause
Lock the elbows in place — forearm only. Full extension at the bottom, squeeze hard. Rest-pause to extend past failure.
Mark done
Overhead cable extension
1 × 10–12 working
Slow neg
The long head only reaches full stretch overhead — largest of the three heads. Control the negative carefully to protect the elbow joint.
Mark done
Thursday
Legs + Calves
The hardest session. The pre-exhaust sequence (leg extension → leg press → hack squat) neurologically engages the quad first so it — not your lower back — becomes the limiting factor.
Quadriceps — pre-exhaust
Leg extension
1 × 15 warm-up
1 × 10–12 working
Pre-exhaustSlow neg
Plantarflex at the top (toes pulled up) for a harder quad contraction. 3-sec lowering. Do not skip this — it determines whether your quads or lower back fail first on the press.
Mark done
Quadriceps — compound
Leg press
2–3 escalating warm-up
1 × 8–10 working
Rest-pauseSlow neg
Feet shoulder-width, full depth. Don't lock out at the top. Rest-pause: unload, breathe 10 sec, grind out 2–3 more.
Mark done
Hack squat
1 × 10–12 working
Slow neg
Higher foot placement emphasises VMO and quad sweep. Slow eccentric. Finishes the quads after the leg press has done significant damage.
Mark done
Hamstrings
Lying leg curl
1 × 12 warm-up
1 × 10–12 working
Slow negRest-pause
Plantarflex (toes away) at the top for a fuller contraction. 3-sec lowering phase. Rest-pause to extend the set.
Mark done
Stiff-leg deadlift
1 × 8–10 working
Slow neg
Feel the hamstring stretch — not the lower back. Soft knee throughout. This is a stretch-and-contract movement, not a strength lift. Go lighter than you think.
Mark done
Calves
Standing calf raise
1 × 15 warm-up
1 × 12–15 working
Slow negRest-pause
Full stretch at the bottom, 1-sec pause. Don't bounce — it bypasses the muscle. Train calves with the same intensity as every other bodypart.
Mark done
Friday
Pectorals + Biceps
Upper chest leads — flat-only pressing builds a shelf, not a complete pec. Incline barbell is your anchor movement. Biceps finish; they've had significant indirect load from Monday.
Pectorals — upper
Incline barbell pressmust
2 × escalating warm-up
1 × 8–10 working
Rest-pauseSlow neg
30–45° incline. Full stretch at the bottom, explosive drive, 3-sec lowering. Rest-pause: re-rack, breathe, push past failure. The primary strength movement of the session.
Mark done
Pectorals — mid + full
Machine incline press
1 × 8 warm-up
1 × 8–10 working
Rest-pauseSlow neg
Machine is safer at true failure without a spotter. Flat DB gives more stretch. Goal is maximum inroad after the incline barbell has taxed the upper chest.
Mark done
Cable crossover
1 × 10–12 working
Slow negForced reps
Hold the peak contraction 1–2 sec. The pec is already exhausted — you won't need much weight. Forced reps work well here.
Mark done
Biceps
EZ bar curl
1 × 10 warm-up
1 × 8–10 working
Slow neg
Strict form — no body English. Biceps are pre-fatigued from Monday rows and pullups. Control the negative on every rep.
Mark done
Incline dumbbell curl
1 × 10–12 working
Slow negRest-pause
Incline position puts the long head under maximum stretch. Full hang at the bottom, slow lowering. Rest-pause to extend past failure.
Mark done
Wednesday
Posture, Mobility + Abs
Focus is hip flexor and thoracic release plus a short but intense abs block. Abs work is anti-extension and anti-rotation — directly addressing pelvic tilt and core stability.
Total: ~45 min
Equipment: mat, foam roller, wall, cable/band
Foam roll — 8 min
Thoracic spine roll
60–90 sec
Thoracic stiffness
Roller perpendicular to spine at mid-back. Support your head, arms crossed. Slowly roll from mid-back to upper back. Pause on tight spots 5–10 sec. Don't roll the lower back.
Mark done
Hip flexor / quad roll
60 sec each side
Anterior pelvic tilt
Face down, roller under one quad/hip flexor. Prop on forearms, slowly roll from hip crease to just above knee. Pause on tight spots.
Mark done
Lat roll
45 sec each side
Rounded shoulders
Side-lying, roller under the armpit/lat. Slow roll down the side of the ribcage. Essential after heavy rowing and pullup sessions.
Mark done
Hip flexor + pelvis — 12 min
90/90 hip stretch
2 min each side
Hip misalignment · Anterior pelvic tilt
Sit with front leg at 90° and rear leg at 90° behind. Sit tall. Hold passively 60 sec, then actively rotate toward the front shin for another 60 sec. Most effective hip drill for your issues.
Mark done
Kneeling hip flexor stretch with posterior tilt cue
90 sec each side
Anterior pelvic tilt
Kneeling lunge. Before leaning forward — squeeze the glute of the back leg and tuck the pelvis under. The tilt is the key. Feel it deep in the front hip, not the lower back.
Mark done
Dead bug
3 × 8 reps
Anterior pelvic tilt
Lie on back, arms up, knees at 90°. Press lower back into floor throughout. Slowly extend opposite arm and leg without letting the back arch. Teaches the core to hold neutral pelvis under load.
Mark done
Abs — 10 min
Hollow body hold
3 × 20–30 sec
Anti-extension · Anterior pelvic tilt
Press lower back into floor, brace core. Extend arms overhead and legs out low — hold without the back arching. Start with legs higher and work them lower over weeks.
Mark done
Ab wheel or cable rollout
3 × 8–10 reps
Anti-extension · Core strength
Start from knees if needed. Roll out slowly, brace hard, return under control. Never let the lower back sag. Most effective anti-extension movement.
Mark done
Pallof press hold
3 × 20 sec each side
Anti-rotation · Hip alignment
Cable or band at chest height, stand side-on. Press the handle straight out and hold — resist the rotation the entire time. Don't let the hips or shoulders rotate toward the cable.
Mark done
Thoracic + shoulder — 10 min
Thoracic extension over roller
10 reps per segment
Thoracic stiffness
Roller perpendicular to spine. Hands behind head, slowly extend back over the roller. Move up one segment at a time. Restoring extension range lost from sitting and pressing.
Mark done
Wall pec stretch
90 sec each side
Rounded shoulders
Arm at 90° against wall, rotate body away. Feel this in the pec minor and anterior delt — not the shoulder joint. Lower the arm angle if you feel joint discomfort.
Mark done
Band pull-apart
3 × 15 reps
Rounded shoulders
Pull shoulders back and apart, squeezing mid-traps and rear delts. Counteracts internal rotation from pressing. Do these daily if you have a band at home.
Mark done
Neck — 5 min
Chin tuck (cervical retraction)
3 × 10 reps, 5 sec hold
Forward head
Draw the chin straight back — not down. Hold 5 sec, release. Strengthens the deep cervical flexors. Do these daily.
Mark done
Neck flexor endurance hold
3 × 20–30 sec
Forward head
Lie on back. Lift head 2–3cm off the floor, chin tucked. Hold for time. Trains deep neck flexors under load — root cause of forward head posture is weakness here, not just tightness.
Mark done
Saturday / Sunday
Active Mobility + Abs
Active mobility moving through ranges rather than just holding. Slightly more demanding abs block — progressing from Wednesday into heavier loaded and dynamic core work.
Total: ~50 min
Equipment: mat, foam roller, band, cable machine
Foam roll — 8 min
Full lower body roll
8 min total
Hip misalignment · Anterior pelvic tilt
Glutes (figure-4 on roller), IT band (side-lying), quads and hip flexors (face down). Spend longer on the tighter side — hip asymmetry usually means one side needs significantly more work.
Mark done
Active hip mobility — 12 min
Hip CARs (controlled articular rotations)
5 circles each direction per hip
Hip misalignment
Standing on one leg, slowly rotate the free hip through full range — forward, out, back, in. As slow as possible. Maps available range on each side, exposing asymmetries.
Mark done
World's greatest stretch
5 reps each side
Hip misalignment · Anterior pelvic tilt · Thoracic stiffness
Deep lunge, same-side hand inside foot. Rotate opposite arm toward ceiling, follow with eyes. Return, then drive knee out to open the hip. Hits hip flexors, thoracic rotation, and hip external rotation in one movement.
Mark done
Pigeon pose or figure-4 stretch
2 min each side
Hip misalignment
Focus on the tighter side. Breathe into the stretch; exhale and release deeper each breath. There is almost always a dominant tight side — find it and prioritise it.
Mark done
Abs — 12 min
Hanging knee raise or leg raise
3 × 10–15 reps
Lower abs · Hip flexor control
Hang from a pullup bar. Tuck the pelvis under before raising the knees — this turns it from a hip flexor exercise into an ab exercise. Raise slowly, lower under full control.
Mark done
Cable crunch
3 × 12–15 reps
Upper abs · Core strength
Kneeling, cable behind head. Curl the thorax toward the pelvis — not a hip hinge. Keep hips stationary. Add weight over time — abs respond well to progressive overload.
Mark done
Pallof press — half-kneeling
3 × 12 reps each side
Anti-rotation · Hip alignment
Half-kneeling (one knee down, same side as cable). Press out, hold 1 sec, return. Down knee on the tighter hip side. Increases demand on the hip stabilisers directly.
Mark done
Plank with shoulder tap
3 × 10 taps each side
Anti-rotation · Core stability
High plank. Tap one shoulder at a time keeping hips completely still — no rotation, no shifting. Squeeze glutes and brace the entire core. If the hips rock, go wider with the feet.
Mark done
Thoracic rotation — 8 min
Seated thoracic rotation
10 slow reps each side
Thoracic stiffness
Sit cross-legged. One hand behind head, the other on knee. Rotate toward the bent elbow, leading with eyes. Isolate the thoracic — don't let the lumbar rotate. 3 sec in, pause, 3 sec return.
Mark done
Open book stretch
10 reps each side
Thoracic stiffness · Rounded shoulders
Side-lying, knees stacked at 90°, arms out front. Rotate top arm back toward the floor behind you, following with eyes. Let the shoulder blade open. Hold end range 2 sec.
Mark done
Neck + posture activation — 5 min
Wall angel
3 × 10 reps
Rounded shoulders · Thoracic stiffness
Back against wall, lumbar and thoracic flat, chin tucked. Arms at 90° against wall. Slowly raise arms overhead keeping full contact. If you can't keep contact — that's your baseline. Work toward it over weeks.
Mark done
Chin tuck + neck nod
3 × 10 reps
Forward head
Chin tuck first (retract), then nod the chin slightly down. Combines deep cervical flexor activation with upper cervical flexion. Small movements, big effect over time.
Mark done
Breath work — 5 min
90/90 breathing (crocodile breath)
5 min
Anterior pelvic tilt · Thoracic stiffness
Lie on back, knees at 90° on a chair. In through nose 4 sec expanding ribcage 360°. Exhale fully 6 sec. Restores diaphragm function, reduces anterior pelvic tilt, opens the thorax. Do this last — natural wind-down.
Mark done
